How to Style and Care for Vintage Kilim & Suzani Textiles

bkCAMUR Home · 7 min read

Vintage textiles reward a little care in a way that mass-produced decor doesn't. A synthetic throw pillow doesn't really age — it just wears out. A hand-woven kilim or a silk-thread suzani, on the other hand, has already survived decades, and with the right handling it can easily outlast anything you'd buy new. Here's how we recommend styling and caring for these pieces, wherever you're decorating in the US.

Styling Kilim & Suzani Pillow Covers

The single easiest way to bring a vintage textile into a room is a pillow cover, and it's forgiving of almost any home style — we've seen these work in mid-century modern living rooms, Scandinavian-leaning bedrooms, farmhouse dens, and full-on maximalist eclectic spaces alike, from coast to coast.

Mix patterns, but ground them

A kilim pillow's geometric weave and a suzani's floral embroidery can absolutely sit next to each other on the same sofa — the trick is grounding the mix with solid-color furniture or a neutral sofa base. Let the textiles be the pattern; keep the upholstery quiet.

Layer sizes, not just colors

Pair an 18x18" cover with a 20x20" and a lumbar shape rather than three matching squares. Vintage pieces are already one of a kind, so let the arrangement look collected rather than matched.

Don't limit them to the sofa

A single suzani cushion on a reading chair, two kilim covers flanking a headboard, or one oversized piece anchoring a window seat all work — these textiles were made to be used, not just displayed.

A single hand-embroidered suzani piece can anchor an entire seating area.

Layering Rugs and Kilims

Rug-on-rug layering has become a genuine design trend for a reason: it's an easy way to add warmth, define a seating area within an open floor plan, and give a flatwoven kilim a job to do even in a room that already has wall-to-wall carpet or a larger neutral rug underneath.

  • Entryways: a smaller kilim over a jute or sisal base rug adds color right where guests first step in.
  • Living rooms: layer a kilim at an angle over a larger neutral rug, positioned under a coffee table to anchor the seating area.
  • Bedrooms: a kilim runner at the foot of the bed adds texture underfoot without competing with bedding patterns.

Caring for Vintage Wool & Silk Textiles

This is the part that actually protects your investment, and it's simpler than it sounds.

Do

  • Vacuum on low suction, or use a hand-held brush attachment, to lift dust without pulling at fibers
  • Rotate pillow covers and rugs periodically so wear and any light exposure is even
  • Spot clean spills immediately with a barely damp cloth and mild soap, blotting rather than rubbing
  • Store flat or rolled (never folded long-term) if putting a rug away seasonally
  • Have wool rugs professionally cleaned by a specialist familiar with vintage or antique textiles, not a standard carpet-cleaning service

Don't

  • Leave suzani embroidery in direct, harsh sunlight for extended periods — silk thread dye can fade faster than the woven base fabric
  • Machine wash or use a steam cleaner on hand-embroidered or hand-woven pieces
  • Fold a rug tightly for long-term storage, which can crease and weaken the foundation threads
  • Use harsh stain removers or bleach on natural dyes, which can shift or strip color unpredictably

A Few Styling Combinations to Try

If you're not sure where to start, these three pairings are a reliable starting point:

  1. The reading nook: one suzani lumbar pillow + a small kilim rug underfoot + a warm wood chair
  2. The layered sofa: two mismatched kilim pillow covers in the same warm palette + one suzani accent pillow in a contrasting floral
  3. The entry statement: a vintage kilim rug layered over jute, styled with a single suzani-covered bench cushion

None of these require matching everything perfectly — that's the whole point of decorating with vintage, one-of-a-kind pieces instead of a matched set.

— The bkCAMUR Home team, Philadelphia
